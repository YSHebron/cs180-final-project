Hi. We are Group 15.

And this is our project, Exploring the Relationship between Air Quality and COVID-19 Infection Rates, it aims to explore the relationship between Air Quality and COVID-19 infection rates. Two separate datasets (Air Quality and Covid-19 Cases) are merged into one and their relationship is explored with the use of Linear Regression.

Here's an overview of our project.

According to WHO in 2018, roughly 7 million premature deaths can be attributed to air pollution each year, with ninety percent of the world’s population continuing to breathe in dangerous levels of air pollutants every day. SARS-CoV-2 is the causative agent of COVID-19 whose spread has reached pandemic proportions. Although the disease no longer constitutes a public health emergency of international concern, periodic surges and new variants continue to pose a threat to everyday life (Taylor & Diamond, 2023). Air pollution and COVID-19 appears to be related, and verifying the existence or non-existence of this relationship would be aided by imposing an air pollution dataset over a COVID-19 dataset, and then checking the accuracy of the model’s predictions.

Hypothesis

There is a significant relationship between COVID-19 and Air Pollution

Null Hypothesis

There is no significant relationship between COVID-19 and Air Pollution

Action Plan

Linear Regression determines the relationship between datasets
